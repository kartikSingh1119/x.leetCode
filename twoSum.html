In the Two Sum problem, we have an array that contains lots of numbers. These numbers can represent anything such as:
  • Prices of items
  • Prices of equipment in games
  • Transactions of a user
  • Prices of gifts
  • Weights of products in a transport line
Now, the main challenge in front of us is finding two numbers from this array whose sum is equal to our target value. Here, the target value can represent anything:
  • It can be our shopping budget
  • It can be the transaction limit of our bank account
  • It can be the weight limit of products in a transport line
  • It can be the price limit of equipment in games
Real World Scenario:

Suppose we are in a shop with ₹500 in our pocket, and we have to buy two items whose prices together are equal to our budget — ₹500.


Now, in this case, we use the Two Sum problem to find those two items whose total price equals our budget.

Using the Two Sum problem for this scenario:

Step 1:

Here we create an array named itemPrice which stores the prices of the items available in the shop:

itemPrice = [100, 80, 230, 210, 120, 400]

Step 2:

We know our budget is ₹500, which will act as our target value.

targetValue = 500

Step 3:

Now we apply the Two Sum problem to solve this and find the two item prices that add up to our target value.

Solution:

100 + 400 = 500 = target value

Note
Here we have a very small list, so we can easily find our target value. But think — when we have thousands or lakhs of items, what do we do then?
That’s where we use the Two Sum problem....

Examples for understand....
Example 1: Gaming / In-Game Purchases
  • Scenario: A mobile game wants to suggest bundles of items that total exactly a player’s in-game currency to maximize engagement.
  • Example: Player has 1,000 coins. Available items: [300, 400, 600, 700]. Suggested pair: 300 + 700 = 1,000 ✅
  • Benefit: Increases player satisfaction by using all currency. Encourages buying multiple items → higher revenue for the game.
Example 2: E-commerce / Discounts
  • Scenario: A shopping app wants to suggest pairs of products that a customer can buy together to reach a discount threshold.
  • Example: If the discount applies for ₹1,000 purchases, and the cart has items ₹400, ₹600, ₹300, ₹700, the app can suggest buying ₹400 + ₹600 = ₹1,000 to get the discount.
  • Benefit: Improves upselling and customer experience.
Example 3: Inventory Management
  • Scenario: A warehouse wants to bundle products into boxes without exceeding weight limits.
  • Example: If the box limit is 50kg, and products weigh 20kg, 30kg, 25kg, 15kg, the system can find combinations like 20kg + 30kg = 50kg.
  • Benefit: Optimizes packing and shipping.
Example 4: Event Planning / Budgeting
  • Scenario: Organizers have a fixed budget for gifts or resources and want to pair items without exceeding the budget.
  • Example: If the budget is ₹1,200, and items cost ₹700, ₹500, ₹300, ₹900, the system can suggest ₹700 + ₹500 = ₹1,200.
  • Benefit: Ensures budget efficiency and planning.
