x. Subarray Sum Equals K Problem

In the Subarray Sum Equals K problem, we have an array that contains many numbers. These numbers can represent anything that keeps changing over time, such as:
  • Daily bank transactions
  • Daily shopping expenses
  • Calories burned each day in a fitness app
  • Sales made by a store every day
  • Room capacities in a hotel
The main goal is to find a continuous part of this array (called a subarray) whose total sum is exactly equal to a target value (K). Here, this target value (K) can represent:
  • A total budget limit
  • A target sales number
  • A calorie goal
  • Or in this case ‚Äî total family members that need to fit into rooms!
Real World Scenario:

Imagine you and your family arrive at a hotel. There are multiple rooms available on the same floor, and each room has a different capacity ‚Äî meaning how many people it can accommodate.

For example, one room can hold 2 people, another 5, another 3, and so on. Your family has a total of 15 members ‚Äî this is your target (K). Now, you want to find a set of continuous rooms whose combined capacity equals exactly 15, so that your whole family can stay together on the same floor without any gaps or other guests in between.

That‚Äôs exactly what the Subarray Sum Equals K problem helps us find ‚Äî a continuous part of data that adds up perfectly to the required total.


Step 1:

We create an array named roomCapacity which stores how many people each room on that floor can hold:

roomCapacity = [2, 3, 4, 6, 5, 3, 2, 4, 1]

Step 2:

The total number of family members is 15. This becomes our target value (K).

targetValue = 15

Step 3:

Now we apply the Subarray Sum Equals K logic to find a continuous group of rooms whose combined capacity is exactly 15.

Solution:

Continuous rooms found:
[4, 6, 5] = 15 = target value

So, your family can book these 3 continuous rooms together (room 3, 4, and 5). This ensures that all 15 members stay together without any other guests in between.

Benefit:

Helps find the perfect continuous combination (like rooms, transactions, or products) that matches an exact total value ‚Äî saving time and ensuring the best arrangement without interruptions.
